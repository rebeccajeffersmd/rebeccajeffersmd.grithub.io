What This Series Covers

This free educational series explains the physiology behind persistent fatigue when labs appear “normal.”

Most people are given isolated pieces of the puzzle — sleep, stress, diet, hormones — without understanding how those systems interact across the day.

  • How daily cortisol rhythm shapes energy from morning through night
  • What “normal” thyroid labs do — and do not — tell you
  • Why sleep quality matters just as much as sleep duration
  • How food timing and composition affect focus, cravings, and crashes
  • How movement timing influences metabolic efficiency and energy

The goal is not quick fixes or hype. It is to help you better understand what may actually be driving how you feel.

ABOUT THE PHYSICIAN

Rebecca Jeffers, MD

I’m a board-certified endocrinologist who works with patients whose symptoms often do not match their lab results.

I created this series because there is rarely enough time in a standard visit to fully explain how sleep, stress physiology, metabolism, hormones, and daily rhythms interact.

The goal is not to diagnose or replace medical care. It is to provide a clearer understanding of the patterns that may be affecting how you feel day to day.

Medical Disclaimer: This website and video series are for general educational purposes only and do not constitute medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. They are not a substitute for care from your personal physician or qualified health professional.
