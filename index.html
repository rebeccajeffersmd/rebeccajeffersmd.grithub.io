What This Series Covers

Created by Rebecca Jeffers, MD — Board-Certified Endocrinologist

This free educational series explains the physiology behind persistent fatigue when labs appear “normal.”

Most people are given pieces of the puzzle—sleep, stress, diet, hormones—but not how those systems actually interact across the day.

That missing connection is often why symptoms continue even when nothing obvious appears “wrong.”

  • How daily cortisol rhythm shapes energy from morning through night
  • What “normal” thyroid labs do—and don’t—tell you
  • Why sleep timing matters just as much as sleep duration
  • How food timing and composition affect focus, cravings, and crashes
  • How movement timing influences metabolic efficiency and energy

The goal is not quick fixes or hype—it’s helping you better understand what may actually be driving how you feel.

About Dr. Rebecca Jeffers

I’m a board-certified endocrinologist who works with patients whose symptoms often don’t match their lab results.

I created this series because there’s rarely enough time in a standard visit to fully explain how sleep, stress physiology, metabolism, hormones, and daily rhythms interact.

The goal is not to diagnose or replace medical care. It’s to provide a clearer understanding of the patterns that may be affecting how you feel day to day.
